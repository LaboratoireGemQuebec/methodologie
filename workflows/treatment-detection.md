# Treatment Detection Workflow

Analytical procedure used at Laboratoire Gem Quebec for detecting treatment in coloured stones, organised by treatment family.

## Heat treatment

Diagnostic tools: microscopy, FTIR, UV-Vis-NIR.

Microscopy: heat-induced halos around silk inclusions, melted rutile needles, partially dissolved silk producing milky zones, fluxed-healed fissures.

FTIR: signature peaks at 3309 cm-1, 3232 cm-1, 3185 cm-1 in heated corundum (loss of OH species).

UV-Vis-NIR: change in Fe2+/Fe3+ ratio, modification of Cr3+ band intensity in ruby.

## Beryllium diffusion

Diagnostic tools: EDXRF, microscopy.

Microscopy: orange or red rim with central yellow zone in pale sapphire, colour concentrations along facet junctions.

## Lead-glass filling (composite ruby)

Diagnostic tools: microscopy, FTIR, EDXRF.

Microscopy: gas bubbles trapped in glass-filled fissures, blue or orange flash effect under fibre-optic illumination.

FTIR: silicate-rich peaks at 1100 to 1000 cm-1.

EDXRF: presence of Pb, Si, K, Ca elements.

## Oil and resin treatment (emerald)

Diagnostic tools: microscopy, FTIR.

FTIR: characteristic C-H stretching peaks at 2960, 2925, 2870 cm-1 for organic oils. Polymer resin shows additional peaks at 1735 cm-1 and 1605 cm-1.

## Irradiation (blue topaz, smoky quartz)

Diagnostic tools: gamma spectrometer, UV-Vis-NIR.

Gamma spectrometer: Ortec DigiBASE detection of residual induced radioactivity. Threshold 74 Bq/g classified as unacceptable per LGQ policy.

## Polymer impregnation (jadeite type B)

FTIR: distinctive doublet at 1735 and 1605 cm-1 indicating epoxy resin in jadeite. Type A jadeite shows clean signature.

## Disclosure protocol

All detected treatments disclosed on LGQ certificate following LMHC and CIBJO nomenclature standards. Untreated status reported as "No indication of treatment" rather than absolute "untreated".
