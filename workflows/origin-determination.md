# Geographic Origin Determination Workflow

Analytical procedure used at LGQ for determining country of origin of coloured stones, primarily ruby, sapphire, and emerald.

## Scope and limitations

Origin determination is offered as a Tier 4 service for ruby, blue sapphire, and emerald. Reported as probabilistic statement (consistent with origin X) rather than absolute, consistent with international laboratory practice (SSEF, GRS, AGL, Gubelin, GIA).

## Sequence

### 1. Microscopy (Nikon SMZ1270)

Document inclusion suite, growth pattern, colour zoning. Specific assemblages diagnostic:

- Burmese ruby: silk three directions, dense rutile needles, calcite, fluxed-healed fissures.
- - Mong Hsu ruby: blue cores, dense flux residues.
  - - Thai/Cambodian ruby: angular almandine garnet inclusions, basaltic.
    - - Sri Lankan ruby/sapphire: long fine silk, zircon halos, milky bands.
      - - Madagascar ruby: zircon halos, dense fingerprints.
        - - Mozambique ruby (Montepuez): minimal silk, transparent zones, basaltic affinity.
          - - Kashmir sapphire: milky velvet appearance from sub-microscopic exsolution.
            - - Burmese (Mogok) sapphire: silk three directions, calcite, mica, apatite.
              - - Sri Lankan sapphire: silk, zircon halos, fluid feathers.
                - - Australian sapphire: dark Fe-rich, basaltic.
                  - - Colombian emerald: three-phase inclusions (gas, liquid, solid), pyrite cubes.
                    - - Brazilian emerald: two-phase fluid inclusions, mica, biotite.
                      - - Zambian emerald (Kafubu): two-phase, dark mica, blue-green hue.
                       
                        - ### 2. UV-Vis-NIR (Magilabs GemmoSphere, 250-1000 nm)
                       
                        - - Burmese ruby: strong Cr3+ at 405 and 555 nm, lower Fe3+ at 450 nm.
                          - - Thai ruby: stronger Fe3+ pattern, iron-rich.
                            - - Kashmir sapphire: low Fe-Ti pair, weak 880 nm Fe2+/Ti4+.
                              - - Australian sapphire: strong Fe2+/Ti4+ at 880 nm.
                               
                                - ### 3. EDXRF elemental (Bruker Tracer 5i)
                               
                                - Trace-element profile primary discriminator:
                               
                                - - Burmese ruby: low Fe (under 1500 ppm), elevated Mg-Ti, low V.
                                  - - Thai ruby: high Fe (over 4000 ppm), low Mg.
                                    - - Mozambique ruby: high Fe (over 5000 ppm), basaltic.
                                      - - Kashmir sapphire: distinctive Ga-Mg ratio, low Fe (under 1000 ppm).
                                        - - Burmese sapphire: low Fe, low Ga.
                                          - - Australian sapphire: high Fe (over 4000 ppm).
                                            - - Colombian emerald: low Mg, low alkalis, schist-affinity.
                                              - - Brazilian emerald: moderate alkalis, mica-rich pegmatite.
                                                - - Zambian emerald: high alkalis, schist-affinity but distinct from Colombian.
                                                 
                                                  - ### 4. Cross-reference databases
                                                 
                                                  - LGQ maintains internal reference comparisons against published patterns from SSEF, GRS, AGL, Gubelin, GIA. Proprietary quantitative algorithms not claimed.
                                                 
                                                  - ### 5. Reporting
                                                 
                                                  - Confidence level: high, moderate, low. Mineralogical and geochemical evidence supporting the assignment. LMHC harmonised origin nomenclature.
